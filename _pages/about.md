I'm an undergraduate student who is currently studying different elements of the vast world of computer science. Some of my interests as of right now are in the field of data science and AI. I look forward to using what I learn to create projects that are sustainable & useful. I'm also minoring in Asian-American studies & mathematics.

I have experience in Python, C++, R & some Java. I also have experience in relational database systems like PostgreSQL & mySQL, a necessary requisite for deploying applications.

I recently interned at MetLife, where I was part of a team consisting of talented data scientists. There, I learned how to use Azure DevOps for Machine Learning to improve my team's playbook by learning what ML pipelines are, and optimizing the playbook code. Working while following the Agile methodology helped me become a productive team member that [mostly] followed a logical flow to meet deadlines. I also possess a Google certification in <a href="https://www.credly.com/badges/b78352dd-fd4f-4741-a1ac-967c8aeb12c7/">Data Analytics</a>, which reinforced my ability to make data-driven choices while simultaneously improving my data science skills.

Currently, I'm working on two projects: <strong>Cup of Joe</strong> [webapp that lets you track how much money you spend on coffee and offers money-saving alternatives] & a fork of my old <strong>NY Population Analysis</strong> program I made as a college freshman [the difference now is I'll incorporate concepts from the different topics of CS that I've learned since then].

As an undergraduate student, I've worked multiple jobs to support my university while making meaningful connections. Some roles include: being an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant (UTA) for 2 courses, College Assistant (SEEK) & Mentor (HCAP). I'm also a student senator in the Hunter College Senate, where I attend meetings alongside fellow senators to vote for changes that improve the college for current/future students & staff.

Special thanks to my friends, mentors & professors; their teachings on not just academia but life, has been invaluable.
