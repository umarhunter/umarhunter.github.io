Umar M. Faruque

Manhattan, NY

I’m an undergraduate student currently studying the science of computers. Some of my interests as of right now are in the field of data science and machine learning. I look forward to using what I learn to create projects that are efficient & useful.

I have experience in Python, C++ and R. I also have experience in relational database systems like PostgreSQL & mySQL, a necessary requisite for deploying applications.

I’m currently interning at MetLife for the second summer where I work alongside a talented team of data scientists and ML engineers. Initially, I learned how to use Azure DevOps for Machine Learning to improve my team’s playbook by learning what ML pipelines are, and optimizing the playbook code. Working while following the Agile methodology helped me become a productive team member that [mostly] followed a logical flow to meet stakeholder demands.

Currently, I’m working on one project: What-A-Match. This project provides a demonstration of different stable matching algorithms that are used worldwide today. These use cases can range from supporting Tinder matches all the way to kidney matchings for patients.

I’m proud to have minored in Asian-American studies where I analyzed historical literature from talented Asian-American authors like Arundhati Roy, Carlos Bulosan, Maxine Hong Kingston, John Okada, Hisaye Yamamoto and many others. These readings have, and always will, continue to shape my own identity.

Special thanks to my friends, mentors & professors; their teachings on not just academia, but life, have been invaluable.
